What Pharos Actually Is

Architecture · Positioning · Differentiation

Pharos is a full-stack parallel Layer 1 blockchain built specifically for real-world financial infrastructure. It is not a general-purpose chain competing on ecosystem size — it is settlement infrastructure designed from the ground up for RWA tokenization, stablecoin rails, and institutional-grade DeFi.

The architecture is built around three layers. L1-Base handles high-performance data availability with hardware acceleration. L1-Core delivers decentralized, high-throughput consensus with subsecond finality. L1-Extension enables Specialized Processing Networks (SPNs), cross-SPN interoperability, and native restaking for heterogeneous financial workloads.

What makes Pharos unusual is its Pharos Virtual Machine — optimized for parallel transaction execution — combined with dual VM compatibility across both EVM and WASM environments. This means developers port existing Ethereum tooling directly while accessing a performance layer that general-purpose EVM chains cannot match.

The founding team is not newcomers to scale. CEO Alex Zhang was CTO at Ant Chain. CTO Meng Wu was Chief Security Officer for Ant Financial's Web3 division. CMO Laura Shen led Solana mobile marketing at Solana Labs. These are engineers who have built and operated payment infrastructure at a scale most blockchain projects only describe in theoretical terms.

The chain targets a Degree of Parallelism at DP4 — combining scalable consensus, parallel transaction execution, full-lifecycle asynchronous pipelining, and high-performance authenticated storage. GPU-inspired architecture is reported to reduce storage requirements by 80% at scale. The target ceiling is 50,000 transactions per second with sub-second finality.

On October 27, 2025, Pharos adopted Chainlink CCIP as its canonical cross-chain messaging infrastructure, alongside Chainlink Data Streams for tokenized RWA markets — completing the oracle integration layer natively rather than as an afterthought.

From Seed to Pacific Ocean

A chronology of milestones
Jul 2024

Seed Round — $8M

Led by Lightspeed Faction and Hack VC. Co-investors include SNZ Capital, Reforge, Dispersion Capital, Hash Global, Chorus One, and others. Pharos begins building.

Mar 6, 2025

Public Devnet Launch

First open testnet. Developers gain access to the modular parallel infrastructure. Builder Base Camp program announced with registrations from March 10.

Mar 7, 2025

$20M Ecosystem Grant Program

Announced to support developers, startups, and institutions building RWA applications, decentralized payments, and financial infrastructure on Pharos.

May 2025

Public Testnet Live

Developers begin building and testing applications. Network activity accelerates throughout the summer.

Oct 27, 2025

Chainlink CCIP Integration

Pharos adopts Chainlink CCIP as its canonical cross-chain messaging infrastructure. Chainlink Data Streams added to support tokenized RWA markets.

Dec 12, 2025

Private Mainnet — Genesis Mint

Genesis supply of 1 billion $PROS minted. Private mainnet launch marks the first milestone in the phased public rollout.

Apr 8, 2026

Series A — $44M

Led by an Asia-based private equity fund, a renewable energy company, and a Hong Kong-regulated financial institution. Sumitomo Corporation Group, SNZ Capital, Chainlink, and Flow Traders participate. Total raised: $52M.

Apr 20, 2026

$PROS Tokenomics Published

1 billion genesis supply. Allocations across foundation, team, investors, and ecosystem. Staking inflation: 0% for months 1–6, then 5% annually.

Apr 28, 2026

Pacific Ocean Mainnet — Public Launch ⚓

Hard fork executed. Public mainnet live. The first asset-native, institution-grade L1 designed for high performance and regulatory compliance is now open to the global DeFi ecosystem.

$PROS serves as the native coin for transaction fees and on-chain execution costs, with potential partial burns as adoption grows. Validators stake $PROS to secure the network and participate in PoS consensus, earning staking rewards in return. Governance rights allow token holders to vote on network upgrades, parameter adjustments, and ecosystem proposals.

Staking inflation is deliberately phased: 0% for the first six months following mainnet launch, rising to 5% annually from month seven. This design minimizes early supply dilution during price discovery while providing a sustainable reward mechanism for validators and delegators at maturity. Future governance may adjust inflation dynamically based on network conditions.
